# Apex Habitat 0.8.5

This release tracks temporary stays and lays the groundwork for idle-resident
accountability. No change to the room generator, maintenance, or other existing
flows.

## What changed for users

- **Temporary stays** — an Accommodation Assignment now has a **Stay Type**
  (Permanent / Temporary) and, for temporary stays, an **Expected Check-out
  Date**. The date is required for temporary stays and cannot precede check-in.
- **Check-out watchlist** — a new daily job flags temporary stays whose expected
  check-out date is approaching, and warns when a worker is still checked in past
  it (posted to the assignment timeline when operational notifications are on). A
  matching disabled-by-default email Notification fires a couple of days ahead.
- **Idle Resident Report (new)** — a tracker for the "forgotten resident"
  problem: a worker living in the camp but not deployed. It captures the
  employee, building, reason (New Hire / Returned / Legal / HR), the responsible
  department, and a status flow (Open → Acknowledged → Resolved). This release
  ships the record and its status rules; the day-counting and automatic routing
  follow in the next release.

## What changed under the hood

- The new field validation and the watchlist job reuse the existing active-stay
  rule (submitted assignment with no check-out) and the proven watchlist job
  pattern (paginated, per-row error isolation, gated timeline comment) — a
  security/stability-conscious, additive change.
- The Idle Resident Report is non-submittable with a controller-enforced status
  flow (no heavy workflow), and rejects a duplicate open report per employee.
- All new text is English-first, translated via `ar.csv`. The **Workspace**
  layout is unchanged; performance is unaffected (no new per-request work).

## If you are updating an existing site

Run `bench migrate` and clear cache after pulling. The new field, settings lead
days, and the disabled Notification are applied automatically. On the next Desk
boot, Frappe shows this update dialog once per user.
