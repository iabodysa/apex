# Apex Habitat 0.8.5

This release lets you book short stays with a clear end date and start holding idle
workers to account. No change to the room generator, maintenance, or other existing flows.

## What changed for users

- **Short stays that stay short** — an Accommodation Assignment now carries a **Stay Type**
  (Permanent or Temporary) and, for temporary stays, an **Expected Check-out Date**, so a
  short stay never quietly becomes permanent.
- **Catch a stay before it overruns** — a new daily watchlist flags temporary residents
  nearing their check-out date and warns when someone is still checked in past it (posted
  to the assignment timeline when operational notifications are on), with an opt-in email
  reminder a couple of days ahead.
- **Spotlight idle workers (new)** — a tracker for the worker living in camp but not yet
  deployed. Capture the employee, building, reason (New Hire, Returned, Legal, HR) and the
  responsible department, and move it through Open → Acknowledged → Resolved. This release
  ships the record and its status flow; day-counting and automatic routing follow next.

## What changed under the hood

- The new field, the watchlist job and the Idle Resident Report build on Apex's proven,
  additive patterns. All new text is English-first, translated via `ar.csv`. The workspace
  layout is unchanged. Security & reliability improvements.

## If you are updating an existing site

Run `bench migrate` and clear cache after pulling. The new field, settings lead days and
the disabled notification are applied automatically. On the next Desk boot, Frappe shows
this update dialog once per user.
