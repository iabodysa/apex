# Apex Habitat 0.8.2

This release adds the Decentralized Internal Store engine and two native Kanban
boards. No change to the room generator, maintenance, or other existing flows.

## What changed for users

- **Internal Store engine** — each Accommodation Building is now its own store.
  Issuing custody to an employee moves stock out of the building store and into
  that employee's custody; returns move it back. Everything is tracked on a new
  read-only **Accommodation Stock Ledger**.
- **Material transfers between buildings** — a new **Accommodation Material
  Transfer** moves Custody Articles and maintenance materials from one building
  store to another: submit ships the stock (In Transit), then **Mark Received**
  lands it in the destination store. The transfer is blocked if the source store
  does not hold enough stock.
- **Finance memo on cross-cost-center transfers** — when stock moves between
  buildings on different cost centers, Finance can be emailed a memo so they can
  record the cross-charge. This is a memo only — no accounting entry is posted.
- **Accommodation Stock Balance report** — shows current on-hand quantity and
  value per building store and per employee custody, reachable from the Custody &
  Asset Control **Workspace**.
- **Kanban boards** — Resident Requests and Maintenance Requests now have
  drag-to-progress Kanban boards for daily triage.

## What changed under the hood

- The store ledger is a signed-quantity ledger with full reversal on cancel, so
  balances stay correct and auditable; postings are idempotent.
- The cross-cost-center memo is opt-in via Habitat Settings and never raises — a
  send failure is logged, protecting submit/receive stability (security and
  reliability hardening). Balance queries are aggregated for good performance.

## If you are updating an existing site

Run `bench migrate` and clear cache after pulling. The Resident/Maintenance
Kanban boards are seeded automatically. On the next Desk boot, Frappe shows this
update dialog once per user.
