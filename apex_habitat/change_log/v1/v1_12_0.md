# Apex 1.12.0

Fuel claims and fuel exception cases now run on guided approval workflows.

## What changed for users

- **Fuel claims follow a clear path.** A claim now moves through **Submit to Movement →
  Reconcile → Approve → Close**, with a status you can read at a glance and a defined step
  for disputes. The person who raises a claim can no longer approve their own.
- **Fuel exception cases are a proper investigation flow.** A case moves through **Open →
  Under Investigation → Evidence Required → Resolved / Rejected → Close**, and a case
  cannot be resolved until the required evidence is in — so issues are closed on the
  record, not by memory.
- **No more silent status edits.** Both documents now change state only through their
  workflow buttons, so the status always reflects a real, permitted action by the right
  role.

## What changed under the hood

- `Fuel Claim` and `Fuel Exception Case` each adopt a native Frappe Workflow (the eighth
  and ninth documents on the Salis approval spine), reusing their `status` field as the
  workflow state with self-approval disabled on the approval and resolution steps.
- The hand-rolled transition checks in both controllers are retired in favour of the
  workflow; required-field validation, the delegation-of-authority gate and the
  evidence-before-resolution control are preserved.

## If you are updating an existing site

Pull and run the database migration; both workflows are seeded automatically. Existing
documents keep their current status and continue from there.
