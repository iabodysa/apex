# Apex 1.1.0

The Salis Movement module gains a metrics layer and a clearer, two-division workspace,
and status colours now come from native Frappe metadata across the app.

## What changed for users

- **Salis opens to a metrics glance.** The Salis workspace now leads with a Key Metrics
  row — active vehicles, vehicles under maintenance, open transport requests, and pending
  approvals — instead of a links-only page.
- **Two-division workspace.** Salis is organised around the Movement department's two
  service lines: a **Workers Transport** section and a **Representatives Fleet** section,
  each with its own dashboard.
- **Two dashboards.** "Salis — Workers Transport" (request status, request type, transport
  over time, demand by project) and "Salis — Representatives Fleet" (vehicles by status,
  fuel spend trend and by platform, rental accrual, dispatch trips) collect the heavier
  charts and cards.
- **Status colours everywhere.** Vehicle, driver, trip, fuel, maintenance and accommodation
  lists now show coloured status indicators that also carry through to Kanban, Calendar and
  Report views.

## What changed under the hood

- Salis now ships **Number Card** and **Dashboard Chart** records as committed fixtures
  (mirroring Habitat), so the metrics import on migrate rather than being created only at
  runtime.
- The 12 custom `*_list.js` indicator scripts were removed in favour of the native DocType
  **States** child table; each status value carries its colour in metadata.
- The Salis workspace is now a navigation hub: a compact metric row plus two division
  sections, with the heavy charts living on the two dashboards.

## If you are updating an existing site

Pull, then `bench migrate` to import the new cards, charts, dashboards and DocType states,
and clear cache. No data migration is required.
