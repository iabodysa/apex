# Apex 1.2.0

Salis grows up. Open it to a manager's-eye view, print the paperwork your team
actually hands over, lean on reports that answer real questions, and let the system
chase the things that used to slip.

## What changed for users

- **A live Dispatch Board.** One screen shows vehicles by status, today's trips, and
  which drivers are free versus already out — so you can dispatch at a glance.
- **Printable documents.** Hand over a vehicle, settle a rental, pay a fuel claim or
  clear a driver with a clean, professional printout.
- **Reports that answer questions.** Vehicle utilisation, fuel spend by vehicle, rental
  cost by office, transport fulfilment SLA, and cost-recovery aging.
- **It reaches out to you.** Salis now raises alerts for expiring vehicle compliance,
  blocked driver clearances, overdue fuel requests and large moves that need sign-off —
  and routes new transport requests and tickets to the right owner.
- **Find your way by area.** Salis is organised into Workers Transport, Representatives
  Fleet, Fuel, Rentals & Costs, Compliance & Drivers, and Setup — each its own space.
- **Deeper dashboards.** More cards and charts per division, so each side of Movement
  shows the numbers that matter to it.

## What changed for managers

- **Approvals you can tune.** The delegation-of-authority tiers and the amount
  thresholds that trigger an Operations sign-off are now settings you control, not fixed
  numbers — adjust them as the company grows.

## If you are updating an existing site

Pull, then `bench migrate` to bring in the new board, reports, documents, dashboards,
workspaces and alerts, and clear cache. No data migration is required.
